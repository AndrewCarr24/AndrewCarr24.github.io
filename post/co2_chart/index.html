This chart displays mean CO2 emissions per mile for the six highest-selling automobile manufacturers in the U.S. For each manufacturer, average CO2 emissions per mile was calculated by taking emissions for each car model and weighting this by the proportion of a manufacturer's revenue coming from sales of that model. For example, if 60% of Honda's revenue came from Accord sales and 40% coming from Civic sales, and the average CO2 associated with Accord and Civic were 350 and 300 respectively, then average CO2 emissions for Honda would be .6*350 + .4*300, or 210 + 120 = 330. To see how much each model contributed to the average emissions of a manufacturer, click on the line associated with that manufacturer.

CO2 emissions data come from fueleconomy.gov and car sales data come from carsalesbase.com.
